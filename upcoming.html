Cumberland Gap (April 7-9)

Our April camp weekend will be a hiking adventure at the Cumberland Gap National Park. The park and its historic offerings along with scenic trails were recently highlighted in February edition of Scouting Magazine. We will depart from the church on Friday 4/7 at 5:30pm with an estimate arrival around 9pm. Our return will be Sunday 4/9 around 3:30pm. The cost per scout/adult will be $35 which covers food and camp fees for the weekend. Parents should provide money (Suggested $7-$10) for lunch on the trip home. Our signup will begin Monday 3/13 and ends Monday 3/27. The size of the backpacking groups is limited to 32 persons, so if your scout is interested please register early.

Location: Cumberland Gap National Park (Wilderness Road Campground, 854 National Park Rd, Ewing, VA 24248)

Camp Dates: 4/7 - 4/9

Cost: $35 Per. Person

Signup Dates: 3/13-3/27

Required Forms: Troop/Crew Permission Slip & Current BSA Medical Form

Our base camp will be in Ewing, Virginia, it will be a group site at the Wilderness Road Campground. For Saturday and Sundays hiking/backpacking groups we will be split in three different groups and traveling over different sections of the park. For those backpacking see attached gear list, a pre trip shakedown will be performed to ensure all scouts have the needed equipment. We do have some backpacking gear available; please contact me with your needs.

Day Hiking Group (New Scouts)

After Saturdays breakfast this group will drive to the Tennessee Park entrance and park in the Daniel Boone parking lot then hike the nearby trails. Areas of interests are as follows; Gap Cave, Iron Furnace and Tri-State Peak to name a few. The group will eat lunch on the trails then return back to base camp for a hearty camp cooked dinner. Scouts and adults should bring a small day packs to carry water, rain gear and a lunch.

Note: The trails we will be hiking have significant elevation changes in places and should be considered strenuous hiking.

Intermediate Backpacking Group

After Saturdays breakfast the Intermediate backpacking group will drive to the Virginia 724 exit off US Hwy 58 then park in the lot near the start of the Ewing Trail head. The group will make their way up to the Ridge Trail passing the White Rocks Overlook and Sand Cave. The group’s final destination for the evening will be the Hensley Back Country Camp. Sunday morning the group will exit the park via the Ridge trail and Ewing Trail for a 7 mile trek back to the parking area.

Note: Estimate distance 9 Miles Saturday and 7 miles on Sunday with and estimated elevation change of 1,655 ft. The majority of the elevation change is over the first 3 miles. See attached list for backpacking gear requirements.

Advanced Backpacking Group (For 14 Year Old Scouts with Experience Backpacking)

After Saturdays breakfast the advanced backpacking group will depart from the Wilderness Road Campground area. The group will make their way via the Lewis Hollow Trail up to the Ridge Trail passing the Skylight Cave, Gibson Gap Campsite and Indian Rock. The group’s final destination for the evening will be the Hensley Back Country Camp or Chadwell Gap Back Country Camps depending on the group size. On Sunday morning the group will exit the park via the Ridge trail and Ewing Trail for a 7 mile trek back to the parking area where the Intermediate backpacking started.

Note: Estimate distance 11.5 Miles Saturday and 7 miles on Sunday with and estimated elevation change of 1,655 ft.

Park Details

Cumberland Gap is situated on the Kentucky-Virginia boundary approximately one-quarter mile north of the point where Kentucky, Virginia, and Tennessee meet. Indians used Cumberland Gap as a gateway through the mountains before the arrival of the white man. In 1750 Dr. Thomas Walker discover Cumberland Gap while searching for a settlement area. In 1775, Daniel Boone lead a group of about 30 men and cut out a road which led through the gap to the mouth of Otter Creek on the Kentucky River.

Ewing trail starts at the Civic Park parking lot and climbs a steep 2.5 miles to White Rocks trail. White Rocks trail short very steep .5 mile trail that leads to the junction of Ridge Trail. Go straight to White Rocks campsite, go right to White Rocks overlook or go left to Sand Cave. The total ascent from the parking lot to the top to White Rocks overlook is 1,655 ' and takes about 2 hrs

The 21 mile Ridge Trail follows along the ridge top of the Cumberland Mountains and leads from the west Pinnacle overlook to White Rocks overlook in the east end of the park. The trail has no visible blazes but does have some mile marker posts along the way. We found that the mile markers and the official map of the area did not match. The trail surface changes from dirt to rock to sand to pebbles and all combinations in between. It is a shared horse trail that is in good condition and is wide. There are many changes in elevation that make this a challenging trail.

There are several features to see while hiking the trail, White Rocks is a large sandstone escarpment that overlooks Poor Valley Ridge. Sand Cave is a large 250 feet across rock house who's sand filled floor is like a east coast beach. A water fall flows over the rock house during wet periods. Martins Fork Cabin has a large stone fireplace, three wooden bunk beds, and a picnic table, and can be rented for $10.00 per night. Be prepared for mice! Hensley Settlement is a restored pioneer settlement (1903-1951) with cabins, farm buildings, schoolhouse, church, spring houses, wooden fences, and family graveyard. Skylight Cave is .4 off the trail and has an opening that lets light in from the top of the cave. We found Indiana brown bats and a small rodent which we could not identify. Pinnacle overlook has a lot of history and was the site of a civil war fort. The overlook sports vistas of Powell Valley, Fern Lake and the city of Middlesboro.

Au Sable River (July 8-13)

We are heading out for a canoe trip to Grayling Michigan where we will canoe, fish, swim, eat, sleep, and have a wonderful time on the beautiful Au Sable River. If you are interested in going on this event, we need commitments by the troop meeting on March 13th. The cost of this trip is $175. A $25 deposit is required by March 13th to hold your spot. The second payment of $75 is due April 17th. The third payment of $75 is due May 15th. Below is the basic itinerary of the trip.

Details:

  • July 8th - Leave early morning to arrive in Dearborn Michigan.
  • July 8th - We will visit the Henry Ford Museum and camp at Camp Munhacke
  • July 9th - We will drive to Grayling Michigan and camp at Hartwick Pines. We will arrive at camp midday and plan on doing some day/afternoon evening hiking.
  • July 10th - We will take a short drive to Penrods Canoe Rental, get our canoes and hit the river! We will camp the night at White Pine.
  • July 11th - We continue our trip on the Au Sable River, camping at Parmalee.
  • July 12th - We get in the canoes and finish out 49th mile on the river, ending at Mio Pond. We will camp at Mio Dam campsite.
  • July 13th - We drive back to Cincinnati.

Costs: The cost of the trip will be $175. There are will be 2 meals that need purchased by the scouts, 1 on the way and 1 on the way back.

Additional Requirements to attend this trip:

  1. All scouts will need to pass the swimmers test in June.
  2. There will be a morning at Camp Friedlander in May where we will witness and make sure that everyone has the required abilities to maneuver the canoes. (required)
  3. We will also have a 1 day local canoe trip (required).
  4. All first year scouts will be required to have a parent with them.
  5. We will not be transporting personal canoes or kayaks. We will most likely rent 1 or 2 kayaks for the scouts to try. We will be transporting all of our gear down the river. Everything will need to be packaged in dry bags. I have posted a few links to help everyone familiarize yourself with the trip.
